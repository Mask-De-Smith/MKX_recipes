Ingredients:

For the chicken:

  • 1.5 lbc (about 7004) boneless skinless chicken thighs (bite sized pieces)
  • 2 eggs (lightly beaten)
  • 1 cup cornstarch
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tbsp white pepper
  • Pinch of salt
  • Oil for deep frying (vegetable, peanut or canola)

For the orange sauce:

  • 1 cup fresh orange juice(or bottled but fresh nonetheless)
  • Zest of 1 large orange
  • 3 tbsp of soy sauce
  • 3 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1/2 cup sugar (or 1/3 cup honey for a richer glaze)
  • 1 tbsp hoisin sauce (optional but adds depth)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger (minced)
  • 1-2 tbsp chilli flakes (adjust for heat)
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch + 2 tbsp water (slurry to thicken)
  • 2 tbsp sesame oil

For garnish:

  • Sliced green onions
  • Toasted sesame seeds
  • Extra orange zest

Instructions:

  1. Prep the chicken coating:
    • In a large bowl, mix cornstarch, flour, baking powder, salt and white pepper.
    • Dip chicken pieces into beaten eggs, then coat them into the dry flour mix. Press gently so the coating sticks.
  2. Fry until crispy:
    • Heat oil in a deep pot or work (~350°F / 175°C)
    • Fry chicken in batches until golden and crunchy (about 4-5 mins per batch). Drain on a wire rack or some paper towels
  3. Making the sauce:
    • In a wok or skillet, add a touch of oil, then sauté garlic, ginger, and chilli flakes until fragrant.
    • Add pramge juice, zest, soy sauce, rice vinegar, hoisin, and sugar. Stir and bring to a simmer.
    • Add cornstarch slurry to thicken into that glossy, sticky glaze until fully coated and shiny.
    • Finish with sesame oil for depth.
  4. Combine chicken + sauce:
    • Toss the crispy fried chicken in the hot orange glaze until fully coated and shiny.
  5. Serve:
    • Plate with steamed or fried rice. Top with sesame seeds, green onions, and maybe extra orange zest for zing.
